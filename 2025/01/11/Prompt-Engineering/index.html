    Posted on 2025-01-11 Edited on 2025-01-13 In LLM Views: Word count in article: 1.3k Reading time ≈ 5 mins.

    The following is the learning note of DeepLearning.AI course: ChatGPT Prompt Engineering for Developers

    Two types of LLMs

    1. Base LLM. Trained to predict the next work based on text training data. Often trained on a large amount of data from sources like the Internet.
    2. Instruction-tunes LLM. Trained to follow instructions and be helpful, honest, and harmless. The training starts off with a base LLM and fine-tunes it with inputs and outputs that are instructions and good attempts to follow those instructions. It is then often further refined using RLHF.

    Principles of prompt engineering

    Principle 1: Write clear and specific instructions

    Principle 2: Give the model time to “think”

    To minimise reasoning errors caused by rushing to an incorrect conclusion, consider reframing the query to request a chain or series of logical steps before the model provides the final answer.

    This approach encourages the model to allocate more computational effort to the task, essentially allowing for a more thorough and accurate reasoning process.

    Model Limitations: Hallucinations

    Hallucinations: Saying something that sounds plausible but not true.

    Hallucinations can be reduced by finding relevant information first then answer the questions based on that information.

    A bonus trick

    You may notice we add a ‘\’ (newline character) to the prompt. Models like GPT-3 isn’t really affected whether you insert newline characters or not. But when working with LLMs in general, you may consider whether newline characters in your prompt may affect the model’s performance.
